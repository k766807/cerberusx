Runtime Authority Architecture

Intelligence proposes. Safety decides.

CerberusX explores layered autonomy for high-risk spacecraft systems. It treats high-capability AI as useful but unsafe with root access, then routes every command through supervision, deterministic boundaries, and audit logs.

GateAuthority manager permits, blocks, revokes, or overrides.
The Three Layers

No single point of judgment.

Layer 1 / The Brain

Strategic Intelligence

Plans, reasons, and adapts. It outputs structured proposals, not direct actuator commands.

Layer 2 / The Critic

Adversarial Watchdog

Audits Layer 1 for telemetry mismatch, suspicious confidence, ignored alternatives, and unsafe reasoning.

Layer 3 / The Brainstem

Deterministic Anchor

Runs small, traceable, non-generative safety rules that cannot be persuaded by intelligent layers.

Research posture: this is a simulation and architecture sketch, not flight software. The strongest claim is that CerberusX exercises authority handoff and deterministic safety-boundary concepts for review and education.
